Rekha's skin care — eczema routine
Pay close attention to her creases: elbows, behind the knees, neck folds. Check every day.
Every morning (after diaper change)
1
Check all skin creases for redness, bumps, or rash
2
If itchy or irritated: apply Hydrocortisone to those areas first. Let it sink in.
3
Follow with a heavy moisturiser: Vani Cream or La Roche-Posay
Every evening (after shower — the most important time)
1
Thorough skin check — this is when you can see everything
2
Apply Mometasone (body) wherever there's a flare-up: bumps, redness, rash in the creases. Thin but generous layer over the whole area.
3
Apply moisturising lotion all over the body
Desonide (face only): Use only if a rash appears on her face. This is used very rarely — it's not part of the daily routine.
Use Cetaphil Baby wash for both girls. Samara also needs lotion after her bath — encourage her to do it!

How the girls play together
Let them play together
You don't need to separate them when there's a disagreement. Help them work it out. Swapping toys or distraction works well.
Offer an alternative, not a yes or no
Instead of "no, you can't have that," try "how about we do this instead?" This works much better with both of them.
Teach turns, not ownership
Both girls understand taking turns. Reinforce this instead of "mine vs yours." For Rekha who is still learning, use the phrase "my turn to borrow." Samara is good at this and can be a role model.

Samara's big questions
Questions about death
This comes up sometimes — it's completely normal for her age. Don't panic or get upset. Acknowledge it simply and move on calmly. (Specific language to use — coming soon.)
When other kids are unkind
We've been teaching her not to escalate. If a child says "we don't want to play with you," she should say: "I'm going to go play with someone else." You can role-play this with her. The goal is calm confidence.
